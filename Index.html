Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Biography

Avul Phakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was a renowned Indian scientist and the 11th President of India. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, in a humble Tamil Muslim family.

Early Life and Education:

Abdul Kalam was the youngest of five siblings. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner and imam of a local mosque, while his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. Kalam had an average childhood, growing up in Rameswaram and studying at theSchwartz Higher Secondary School . He was a bright and hardworking student. In 1954, Kalam graduated with a degree in aeronautical engineering from theMadras Institute of Technology (now part of Anna University). His interest in flight and space technology led him to pursue further studies and research in these fields.

Career:

After completing his education, Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) as a scientist. He later moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). Kalam played a crucial role in the development of India's space and missile programs. He was involved in the design and development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle,SLV-III , which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit. Kalam's contributions to missile technology earned him the nickname "Missile Man of India." He was the project director of India's first indigenous Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit. He also played a pivotal role in the development of ballistic missile technology, notably the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Presidency:

In 2002, Abdul Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. During his tenure, he was known for his humility, accessibility to the common people, and his vision for a developed India. He emphasized the importance of education and healthcare for the country's progress. Kalam was a popular president and was often referred to as the "People's President."

Post-Presidency:

After his presidency, Abdul Kalam continued to be involved in various educational, scientific, and humanitarian initiatives. He was a visiting professor at several academic institutions and played an active role in promoting education among India's youth. Kalam was also a prolific writer and authored several books, including his autobiography "Wings of Fire."

Death:

Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, after suffering acardiac arrest while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong . His death was mourned by the entire nation, and he was given a state funeral with full military honors.

Legacy:

A.P.J. Abdul Kalam is remembered as one of India's greatest scientists and leaders. He inspired millions of youth with his vision, dedication, and commitment to transforming India into a developed nation.
